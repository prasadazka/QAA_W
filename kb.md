# Qatar Aeronautical Academy — Knowledge Base

## 1. About the Academy

### 1.1 Overview
Qatar Aeronautical Academy (QAA) operates under the motto **"Excellence Becomes Reality"**. It is the Gulf region's leading provider of aviation training, offering full-time approved courses for pilots, aircraft maintenance engineers, air traffic controllers, meteorologists, airport operations personnel, and flight dispatchers.

### 1.2 History
- **1927** — First discussions about emergency landing strips near Doha for international flights
- **1952** — Gulf Aviation Company initiated scheduled passenger flights
- **1977** — Founded as the **Civil Aviation College of the Gulf States** (regional cooperative)
- **1996** — Renamed **Qatar Aeronautical College**, reflecting specialized focus and national development alignment
- **Present** — Recognized as Gulf region's leading aviation training provider

### 1.3 Leadership
- **Director General:** Sheikh Jabor Bin Hamad M. Al-Thani
- **Focus:** Outstanding standards in advanced aviation disciplines
- **Mandate:** Grow the aviation sector and transform aviation education culture in Qatar

### 1.4 Vision, Mission & Values

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Vision** | Prepare qualified cadres according to highest international quality standards to achieve leadership in civil aviation |
| **Mission** | Prepare a generation of leaders through improved training opportunities and latest training systems |
| **Values** | Integrity, loyalty, transparency, non-discrimination, teamwork |
| **Core Goal** | Granting diplomas and degrees in Applied Science while collaborating with international education entities |

## 2. Academic Programs

### 2.1 Program Overview

| Category | Discipline | Target Outcome |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| Flight Training | MPL / CPL | Commercial Pilot Certification |
| Engineering | Aircraft Maintenance (AME) | Licensed Maintenance Engineer |
| Navigation | Air Traffic Control (ATC) | Certified Air Traffic Controller |
| Earth Sciences | Meteorology | Specialized Aviation Meteorologist |
| Ground Ops | Flight Dispatch | Licensed Flight Dispatcher |

The academic portfolio is structured into four pillars:
1. **Foundation Programs**
2. **Academic Programs** (full-time)
3. **ICAO Short Courses**
4. **English Language Proficiency** (IELTS)

### 2.2 Pilot Training — Multi-Crew Pilot License (MPL)
- Competency-based training for multi-crew airline cockpits
- Emphasizes human factors, communication, and system-management skills
- Prepares pilots specifically for aircraft like the **Airbus A320**
- Also offers traditional **Commercial Pilot License (CPL)** pathway
- **Instructor requirements:** Professional pilot license (QCAA-accepted), A-320 type rated, airline jet experience, minimum 1,500 hours multi-crew, 1,000 hours on aircraft above 20,000 kg

### 2.3 Aircraft Maintenance Engineering (AME)
- Meets **EASA Part 147** and **QCAA** regulations
- Specializations: **Category B1** (Mechanical) and **Category B2** (Avionics)
- Training on academy fleet: Diamond DA40, DA42, Piper PA28/PA34
- Integrated with QAA's own **MRO department** (EASA & FAA approved)
- Seamless transition from classroom to hands-on maintenance on live aircraft

### 2.4 Air Traffic Control (ATC)
- Trains students in aircraft movement management (ground and air)
- Utilizes advanced surveillance and communication technologies
- Designed to meet **ICAO standards**
- Graduates can operate within any international airspace framework

### 2.5 Meteorology
- Addresses unique climatic challenges of the Gulf region
- Covers thermodynamics, atmospheric physics, satellite imagery, and radar
- Provides accurate weather forecasting and data analysis for flight planning
- Graduates serve airlines and airport operators

### 2.6 Airport Operations Management
- Prepares students for managing large-scale aviation hubs
- Focused on logistics and safety challenges
- Aligned with **Hamad International Airport** standards

### 2.7 Flight Dispatch
- Operates per **ICAO Doc 10106** and **QCAA** regulations
- Flight dispatchers are "pilots on the ground"
- Responsibilities: fuel calculations, weather monitoring, safety compliance
- Curriculum includes **Electronic Flight Bags (EFB)** and advanced flight planning software

## 3. Foundation & Language Programs

### 3.1 Technical Foundation
- **Physics:** General physics to aviation-specific technical physics
- **Mathematics:** Tailored for pilot training and maintenance engineering
- Provides theoretical basis for aerodynamics, propulsion systems, and structural mechanics

### 3.2 English Language Training
- English is the language of international aviation
- Programs offered:
  - **General English**
  - **English for Aviation** (aviation phraseology)
  - **Academic English**
- Prepares students for **ICAO Language Proficiency Requirements (LPRs)**
- Instructors hold MA in TESOL, CELTA, or DELTA certifications

## 5. Admission & Registration

### 5.1 How to Apply
- Applications submitted **in person** at the Registration Department, Al Khulaifat
- **One application policy** — only one program per application (no multiple submissions)
- Applications can be for academic programs or short courses

### 5.2 General Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Identification** | Copies of Applicant ID and Passport |
| **Guardian ID** | Required for all students |
| **Photographs** | 4 passport-size photos, blue background |
| **Registration Fee** | QR 300 (non-refundable, covers placement test) |
| **Placement Test** | Mandatory assessment of academic readiness |

### 5.3 Domestic Applicants (Qatar-Based)
- Must hold a **high school diploma** or equivalent
- Minimum grade: **70%** (varies by program; pilot training requires higher math/science)
- Final-year students may apply with first-semester results (must supply final proof by deadline)

### 5.4 International Applicants
- Must provide certified certificates from **6th, 9th, and 12th grades**
- Certificates must be certified by:
  1. Ministry of Education (home country)
  2. Ministry of Foreign Affairs (home country)
  3. Qatari Embassy in home country
- **Police clearance certificate** from home country (mandatory)
- **Personal interview** required

### 5.5 Post-Application Steps
1. **Medical Examination** — Tailored to specialization (e.g., Class I Medical for pilots)
2. **Security Clearance** — Police Clearance Certificate within the State of Qatar
